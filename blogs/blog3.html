Nutrient Reduction with Mussels

So let's continue with the story of eutrophication.

4) The reason why bivalve molluscs can reduce nutrients is that they eat the algae. Therefore, removing the bivalves from the sea, means removing the eaten algae along with the nutrients. Furthermore, toxic algae are rarely toxic to bivalves. The toxin might affect humans depending on what it is.

5) Of course, some of the nutrients will be returned to the sea as faeces. However, the faeces often sink to the bottom, where it can't cause new algal blooms. On the sea bottom, it is either taken up by the suffering, suffocating lofe at the bottom, helping it, or it is buried.

6) If it is buried, there is also a chance that a certain type of bacteria live in that particular ground. These bacteria have the ability to turn nitrogen from a form, that most algae can use to grow, to a form they can't.

More on 6: Then, the nitrogen is returned to the atmosphere as part of its natural cycle
