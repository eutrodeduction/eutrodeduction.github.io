Nutrient Reduction with Mussels Part 1

1) Phosphorus and nitrogen are so called "nutrients", which causes eutrophication, since the nutrition makes algae grow quicker, while creating a hostile environment with little oxygen for every other creature. This drop in oxygen levels causes the sea bottom to release more phosphorus, which we call internal load. External load refers to tye nutrient runoff from land.

2) Not all types pf algae are bad. The problem is not the algae themselves, but that they take over, and that these algal blooms often are caused by human waste. There are, however, natural, harmless, and good algal blooms worth preserving. Since the solution will take way all algae, we need to be sure that we don't take away the good algae. Luckily, they thrive under different conditions. More nitrogen and phosphorus can be assumed to mean more bad algae.

3) It has been proven succesful to use aquaculture of bivalve molluscs (for example mussels) for nutrient extraction, since they naturally reduce the nutrient levels. It is also easy to harvest them. We want to use common blue mussel and a hybrid, known as baltic m. edilus x m. trossulus, between the blue mussel and the foolish mussel. Blue mussels are less adaptive to harsh environments, but they are valuable in marketing (and have more meat), while the hybrid is more resistent, but has less meat, and is less valuable in marketing. Therefore, both are needed.
